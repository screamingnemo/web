Where It Started

It started in a hotel room in 2020 at the height of COVID-19.

At the time, I was a drunk POS at rock bottom, struggling with addiction—just trying to stay alive and figure out what the hell came next.

Amid all the chaos and uncertainty, of all things, I was intrigued by etymology—especially Latin-rooted words.

Nemo stood out: nobody, nothing, no one. It just felt right.

Since then, I have made large strides in recovering, transforming, and building myself and my life.

Music has always given me something to hold onto, and I will never let it go.

What I Do

I write rock ‘n’ roll songs that pull from the blues, pop, alternative, and whatever else feels right in the moment.

I put words on paper and shape the sound until it matches what’s playing in my head — whatever the feeling.

Most of what I write is rooted in the human condition—the messy beauty of being alive.

The highs, the lows, the good and the bad, and everything else that define what it means to be human.

I make songs that hit hard, hold your hand, move your feet, and help you fall asleep.

Beyond my own music, I create social media content that promotes other bands and artists.

I share simple chord progressions behind well-known songs — the same way I learned.

A strong foundation makes everything else possible, from playing and writing full songs to playing flashy solos.

On livestreams, I dive into current events, technology, society, the world around us, and play video games — creating a space for open conversation.

Why It Matters

Making music has been my way to process life’s ups and downs—I hope my songs help you through the tough times and celebrate the good ones.

We’re all grappling with life’s chaos, making it up along the way through life’s uncertainty.

Music digs into those real, raw feelings and reminds us we’re not alone.

The struggle with pain, doubt, and trying to figure "it" out. Life is messy.

With kindness, compassion, and empathy we each have a part to play in shaping the world.

I believe in human rights, freedom, justice, and the pursuit of happiness for all.

The world doesn’t change by accident. It changes when we decide to show up.

That’s why I donate 50% of the profit from each merch sale to charities and organizations that align with my beliefs and values.

If we want better, it’s on us to make it happen—together.
